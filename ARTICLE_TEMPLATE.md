## Introduction

Open with a hook that establishes why this topic matters RIGHT NOW. Address the reader's pain point directly in the first 2-3 sentences.

State what the reader will learn by the end of this article. Make a clear promise.

> Optional: Use a blockquote for a compelling statistic or key insight early in the article.

## [H2: Core Section 1 — The Problem or Context]

Provide background context. Why does this topic exist? What problem does it solve?

Include factual support: statistics, real examples, or case studies.

### [H3: Subsection — Key Point]

Break down complex ideas into digestible subsections. Use plain language.

Use formatting to improve scannability:

- Bullet point one explaining a concept
- Bullet point two with supporting detail
- Bullet point three wrapping up the idea

### [H3: Subsection — Another Key Point]

Continue developing the topic. Each subsection should have a single, clear focus.

| Column One | Column Two | Column Three |
|---|---|---|
| Data point | Supporting info | Result |
| Data point | Supporting info | Result |

## [H2: Core Section 2 — The Solution or Method]

This is where you deliver the main value. Be specific and actionable.

### Step 1: [Action]

Describe exactly what the reader should do. Use numbered lists for sequential steps.

1. First action with specific detail
2. Second action — include why this matters
3. Third action — mention common mistakes to avoid

### Step 2: [Action]

Continue the step-by-step guidance. Screenshots or examples referenced here if applicable.

### Step 3: [Action]

Complete the core solution. Summarize what the reader has accomplished.

## [H2: Core Section 3 — Advanced Tips or Variations]

Add depth for readers who want to go further. This section separates a good guide from a great one.

- **Pro tip 1:** Specific advanced technique
- **Pro tip 2:** Time-saving shortcut or tool recommendation
- **Pro tip 3:** Common mistake and how to avoid it

## FAQ

**Question 1: The most common question about this topic?**

Answer in 2-3 sentences. Be direct. No fluff.

**Question 2: Second most common question?**

Answer with specifics. Reference any tools, timeframes, or numbers where relevant.

**Question 3: Edge case or nuanced question?**

Acknowledge the nuance, then give the clearest answer possible.

**Question 4: What to do next / follow-up question?**

Bridge to related actions the reader can take.

## Conclusion

Summarize the 3-5 key takeaways from this article in plain sentences (not a bullet list — save that for the body).

End with a clear call to action: what should the reader do right now?
